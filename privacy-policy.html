Privacy Policy

Effective: Nov 15, 2018

Welcome to Blink AI (“Blink AI,” “we,” “us” or “our”). Blink AI enables its client to reconnect the disconnected data and also provides ways to take actionable insights from their own data using AI.

Our Privacy Policy explains how we and some of the companies we work with collect, use, share and protect information in relation to our mobile services, web site integration, and any software provided on or in connection with Blink AI services (collectively, the “Service”), and your choices about the collection and use of your information. We undertake to build the Service and any of its features in such a way that it respects your privacy and allow you not only to understand what we do with your customers personal information, but also manage your data effectively.

By using our Service you understand and agree that we are providing a platform for you to engage with your customers, and drive your customers towards an actionable interactions.

Our Policy applies to all visitors, users, and others who access the Service (“Users”).

1. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We collect the following types of information.

Information your customers provide us directly. If your customers are unregistered user we collect User Content (including Name, Phone number, Facebook Info) that they post through the Service.

Analytics information. We use third-party analytics tools to help us measure traffic and usage trends for the Service. These tools collect information sent by your customers device or our Service, including the web pages they visit, add-ons, and other information that assists us in improving the Service. We collect and use this analytics information with analytics information from other Users so that it cannot reasonably be used to identify any particular individual User.

Cookies and similar technologies. When your customer visits the Service, we may use cookies and similar technologies like pixels, web beacons, and local storage to collect information about how your customer use Blink AI and provide features.

Log file information. Log file information is automatically reported by your browser each time you make a request to access (i.e., visit) a web page or app. It can also be provided when the content of the webpage or app is downloaded to your browser or device.

When your customer uses our Service, our servers automatically record certain log file information, including your web request, Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, browser type, referring / exit pages and URLs, number of clicks and how they interact with links on the Service, domain names, landing pages, pages viewed, and other such information. We may also collect similar information from emails, or messages sent to our Users which then help us track which emails or messages are opened and which links are clicked by recipients. The information allows for more accurate reporting and improvement of the Service.

Metadata. Metadata is usually technical data that is associated with User Content. For example, Metadata can describe how, when and by whom a piece of User Content was collected and how that content is formatted.

Users can add or may have Metadata added to their User Content including a hashtag (e.g., to mark keywords when you share a photo) or other data.

Registered users. For registered user we may collect Personal Data about you including the following:

  • Email/Phone number
  • Name
  • Channel information
  • Vehicle Information
  • Different ID assigned to you when you use the Service (for example, Facebook TAM ID, xTime ID)

If the information covered by this Section is aggregated or de-identified so it is no longer reasonably associated with an identified or identifiable natural person, we may use it for business purpose. To the extent information covered by this Section is associated with an identified or identifiable natural person and is protected as personal data under applicable data protection laws, it is referred to in this Privacy Policy as “Personal Data.” We use pseudonymization for particular types of Personal Data to ensure better security of your Personal Data. Please bear in mind that provisions of Section 3 do not apply to pseudonymized Personal Data.
